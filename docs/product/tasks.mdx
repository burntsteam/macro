Tasks in Macro have been built specifically to avoid superfluous ceremony. Instead they are meant for you to quickly and easily create new ones while closing out old ones.

Because tasks are integrated into the rest of your workspace, there's no need for extraneous labels and categories. It's just status, priority, assignee.

> To draft a new email use shortcut <kbd>c</kbd> \+ <kbd>t</kbd>

<Frame>
  ![Task From Message](/images/task_from_message.png)
</Frame>

You can reference a task in your channels, in your documents, or with an agent by @mentioning it. Quickly create a task directly from a channel or an email by hovering and selecting the task button.

You can manually review all of your teams tasks in the task component or ask your agent to give you updates on them.

Macro tasks can be integrated with github, and are automatically updated to: In progress, In review, or Done as you create, review, and merge branches. They also have context on whose PR ID is associated with which task. You can link your github account in settings under account.
